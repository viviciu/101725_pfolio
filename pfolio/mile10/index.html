Inspired by how tree rings measure growth, Mile 10 etches users sobriety progress into a set of Milestone Discs.

Concrete on the 1 year and 6 month milestones makes it physically tougher to engrave, as it's expected there to be thousands of disc rotations over a long time period. The toughness of the material also represents the resilience of the user.
features
pledge. plug. etch.
When you feel a craving, spin the disc.
Mile 10 permanently, physically etches users' sobriety progress into a Disc as they spin it when they have a craving. Each scratch becomes tangible evidence of the strength given when resisting the urge to smoke. The disc is earned once a sobriety milestone is reached, and users can preserve it as a celebration of their growth and reminder of their resilience.
As you spin it, the nib etches a ring. Each scratch is evidence of the visible strength you gave when resisting the urge to smoke.
nib, in orange.
Every night, you shift the nib closer to the center—one step closer to your goal. Deeper rings show the days that you had the strongest cravings, but resisted—something to you can celebrate even if you break sobriety later. Life happens, and its part of the journey.
display it on a stand, or keep with you always as a reminder of your growth.
trade the shell of your current milestone disc with a friend also quitting—each shell has a barcode with your unique identifier stamped on.
